# Why We Need a Term Sheet — Even Though We're Not Raising VC

> **Audience:** RAV co-founders preparing for incorporation. Share this before the founder meeting where the term sheet gets discussed and signed. **Read time: ~5 minutes.**

---

## The misconception

> *"A term sheet is needed only when we're actively going to venture capital to get funds and bringing somebody else in. We're not doing that. So why are we signing one?"*

This is one of the most common questions when founders see the words "Term Sheet." Fair question. Here's the honest answer.

---

## What a term sheet actually is

A **term sheet** is a written agreement that captures *intent* before *formal documents* are drafted.

Term sheets show up in many contexts:

| Context | Who signs it | What it does |
|---|---|---|
| **VC fundraising** (the famous one) | Investor + Company | Locks in terms before lawyers draft the formal Stock Purchase Agreement |
| **Acquisitions / M&A** | Buyer + Target | Locks in terms before lawyers draft the Definitive Acquisition Agreement |
| **Real estate transactions** | Buyer + Seller | Locks in terms before the formal Purchase & Sale Agreement |
| **Founders' agreement** ← *what we're signing* | The 4 of us | Locks in terms before Stripe Atlas issues our formal Stock Purchase Agreements |

The pattern is the same: **agree first in plain language, then let the lawyers (or in our case, Stripe Atlas's templates) generate the formal version.** The term sheet is what makes sure the formal version isn't a surprise to anyone.

---

## Why we specifically need one for RAV

Here are the five concrete reasons:

### 1. Atlas will issue formal Stock Purchase Agreements at incorporation

When we click "Incorporate" on Stripe Atlas, the wizard generates:
- A Certificate of Incorporation (filed in Delaware)
- Bylaws
- An **individual Stock Purchase Agreement** for each of us
- A 4-year vesting schedule with a 1-year cliff (the standard)
- An indemnification agreement
- Organizational consents

These are **legally binding documents**. The day we sign them, our equity, vesting, and roles are locked in.

**The term sheet is what we agree to BEFORE Atlas drafts those docs.** Without it, the four of us would be negotiating equity percentages while one of us is paying the $500 Atlas fee in real-time, in front of a wizard that demands a number. That's a bad way to make a $X-million decision.

### 2. It surfaces the hard conversations now, when stakes are zero

RAV right now: zero customers, zero revenue, zero outside money. Stakes are theoretical.

RAV in two years: real revenue, real customers, possibly investors looking at us, possibly an acquirer. Stakes are real.

If we can't agree on equity split and vesting today, we definitely can't agree on it after there's actual money on the table. The term sheet forces the four of us to have the difficult conversations now. If we make it through, great — we're aligned. If we get stuck, we'd much rather discover that today than 18 months from now after Sujit has built another year of code or Ajumon has put in 500 hours of operations work.

### 3. It's the "we all agreed to this" document if anything ever goes sideways

We're four friends and we trust each other. That's why we're doing this together.

But here's a real scenario: a co-founder takes an unexpected job, has a family situation, decides this isn't for them, leaves the company in month 9. Without vesting, they'd walk away with their full 25% stake. Without a term sheet documenting that we agreed on vesting, the conversation about clawing back their unvested shares becomes painful, friend-vs-friend.

With a signed term sheet that says "4-year vesting, 1-year cliff, no exceptions for any founder" — the conversation is short. "Per the agreement we all signed in April, you've vested 0% of your stake. Welcome to former-co-founder status with 0 shares." Awkward, but not adversarial. The document does the hard talking.

### 4. Acquirers will ask for it

If RAV is acquired by Marriott Vacations Worldwide / Hilton Grand Vacations / Wyndham / Expedia / Booking Holdings in 3-5 years (which is the explicit exit thesis), the acquirer's M&A counsel will request "founders' equity documentation from inception." Standard due-diligence question.

What they want to see:
- The original Stock Purchase Agreements (Atlas will generate these)
- Any pre-incorporation agreement among the founders that the SPAs were derived from (the term sheet)
- Documentation that all founders signed off on the equity allocation in advance

Without a term sheet, the answer is: "we discussed it verbally and then Atlas's wizard generated the SPAs." That's a yellow flag in due diligence — acquirers want to see paper trails. A signed term sheet eliminates the question.

### 5. It's free protection

This term sheet costs us nothing. It takes ~30-60 minutes to discuss and sign. It doesn't require lawyers (we'll engage lawyers for the platform documents — see the lawyer outreach files). It doesn't lock us in to anything that Atlas isn't already going to lock us in to anyway. And it gives us all five of the benefits above.

The only "cost" is the awkwardness of having the equity conversation in advance. Which we have to have anyway. Doing it now via a structured document is easier than doing it later in a Slack thread.

---

## The clearest analogy

A founder term sheet is like writing down the rules of a board game *before* you start playing for real money.

If everyone agrees on the rules now — when no one has won or lost anything — the game is fun and the rules are easy to write. If you wait until someone is winning or losing, suddenly everyone has a strong opinion about whether that rule is fair, and the conversation gets ugly.

Or another analogy: it's like a prenup, but for a friendship that's becoming a business. Awkward to write down. Invaluable if anything ever happens. And the act of writing it forces useful conversations that would otherwise be avoided.

---

## What this term sheet specifically does NOT do

To be clear, the term sheet **does not**:

- ❌ Issue any actual shares (Atlas does that at incorporation)
- ❌ Make the company exist (Atlas filing does that)
- ❌ Trigger any tax events (those start at stock issuance, which is at Atlas filing)
- ❌ Bind us to outside investors or anyone else (it's just among the 4 of us)
- ❌ Replace formal lawyer review (we're doing that separately for the platform documents)

What it **does** do is document our agreement on:

- Who the four founders are
- What roles we each take (CEO/CTO, COO, CPO, CFO)
- What percentage of founder Common Stock each of us gets
- That we agree to 4-year vesting with a 1-year cliff (Atlas standard)
- That we agree to file our 83(b) elections within 30 days (IRS hard deadline)
- That Ajumon's and Celin's stock issuance is reserved until their OBA disclosures clear
- That all four of us assign our pre-incorporation IP contributions to RAV
- That Sujit will be the initial sole director on the board (at incorporation)

That's it. It's a 4-page document that captures the agreement we'd be making informally anyway, and writes it down so it can't be misremembered.

---

## What happens after signing

1. **Sujit** starts the Stripe Atlas filing process the same week (no later than 7 days after term-sheet signing)
2. **Atlas** generates the formal Stock Purchase Agreements within 7-10 business days
3. **Sujit and Sandhya** sign their formal SPAs and receive their founder stock immediately
4. **Sujit and Sandhya** mail their 83(b) elections within 30 days (HARD IRS DEADLINE — not negotiable)
5. **Ajumon and Celin** wait for OBA disclosures to clear, then sign SPAs and file their own 83(b) elections within 30 days of that signing
6. **All four** sign IP assignment agreements at incorporation (regardless of stock-issuance timing)

The term sheet is step 0 of all of this. Everything else flows from it.

---

## TL;DR

- Term sheets aren't only for VC. They're the "agree-first-then-formalize" pattern used everywhere.
- We need one because Atlas will generate formal Stock Purchase Agreements based on what we tell it. We have to agree among ourselves first.
- It costs nothing, takes an hour, and protects all four of us if anything ever goes sideways.
- It does NOT replace legal review of platform documents (that's a separate engagement we're scoping with a Florida marketplace lawyer).
- Sign the term sheet → start Atlas filing the same week.
