# Equity Split — Briefing for the Founder Meeting

> **Audience:** RAV co-founders. **Read before the meeting.** This is the reading half of the equity-split material; the fill-in form is in `equity-split-worksheet-form.md` (printed copies will be on the table at the meeting).

> **Read time:** ~5-10 minutes.

---

## Background for this decision

Among any group of founders, different people contribute different things at different times. Some put in more hours pre-formation; others ramp up post-formation. Some bring capital, others bring network or domain expertise. Some are full-time from day one; others phase in over time as outside commitments wind down. **None of these is wrong** — each is a real and legitimate way to be a founder.

The equity split we land on should reflect the four of us **collectively agreeing on how to weigh these differences** (or whether to weigh them at all). The right answer might be a flat split, or a weighted split, or something in between — what matters is that we share the same framing before we pick a number.

The shared framing for this meeting: **the four of us know there are differences in time commitment, pre-formation contribution, and current involvement; we accept those differences as normal among founders; and we're choosing together how to recognize them in the equity split.**

With that shared understanding, each founder will fill in the worksheet form independently at the meeting.

---

## The choices we're picking among

Three reasonable splits, each reflecting a different way to weigh contribution differences. **None of these is pre-decided.** Each founder picks the one they find most fair; we discuss as a group; we land on a final number.

### Option A — Equal split (25 / 25 / 25 / 25)

| Sujit | Ajumon | Sandhya | Celin |
|---|---|---|---|
| 25% | 25% | 25% | 25% |

**The view:** All four founders are equal partners in founding equity. Differences in time commitment or contribution can be recognized later through compensation, options, role authority, or other forms of acknowledgment — but not through founding equity itself.

**Pick this if:** You believe founding equity should be flat among co-founders, and contribution differences belong in other forms of recognition.

### Option B — Modest weighted split (30 / 30 / 20 / 20)

| Sujit | Ajumon | Sandhya | Celin |
|---|---|---|---|
| 30% | 30% | 20% | 20% |

**The view:** Two founders are currently more involved in day-to-day work than the other two. The split acknowledges that with a contained 10-point weighting, while keeping the structure simple (top two / bottom two rather than a graduated scale).

**Pick this if:** You want current involvement differences acknowledged in equity, but in a contained, binary way rather than a graduated one.

### Option C — Graduated split (35 / 30 / 20 / 15)

| Sujit | Ajumon | Sandhya | Celin |
|---|---|---|---|
| 35% | 30% | 20% | 15% |

**The view:** A graduated scale where each founder's share more directly tracks their current involvement level. Recognizes that involvement differences exist not just between two groups but on a finer spectrum across all four founders.

**Pick this if:** You believe involvement differences should translate to equity at finer granularity, with each founder's share reflecting their individual contribution rather than grouping into top/bottom buckets.

---

## How to think about your choice

Three questions that help cut through the options:

1. **Founding equity vs other forms of recognition — where should contribution differences show up?** Equal-pickers tend to put contribution differences in compensation, options, or role authority; weighted-pickers put them (also) in founding equity.
2. **Binary acknowledgment vs graduated acknowledgment?** If you want differences acknowledged but contained, lean B. If you want each founder's share to reflect individual contribution levels, lean C. If you want neither, lean A.
3. **What does this look like 18 months from now?** Founders' relative involvement will shift over time — OBA-pending founders clear and ramp up; full-time founders' day-to-day work continues to compound. The split locks in today. Pick the option you believe will still feel right after that shift.

---

## What happens at the meeting

Each founder fills in the worksheet form independently (5-10 min, no discussion), then we reveal all four picks at the same time and discuss.

Bring this briefing with you to the meeting as a reference if you want to re-check the option descriptions while filling in the form.
