## 3. Vesting

All founder Common Stock vests on the following schedule (Atlas default — do not change):

- **Vesting period:** 4 years
- **Cliff:** 1 year (no shares vest until 12 months after the vesting commencement date)
- **Vesting frequency after cliff:** Monthly (1/48 of total grant per month for the remaining 36 months)
- **Acceleration:** None at this stage. (Single-trigger or double-trigger acceleration may be added later when the Company raises outside capital — Atlas's standard documents do not include acceleration by default.)

**Vesting commencement date:** Each Founder's vesting begins on **[the date of their respective Stock Purchase Agreement]**. For Sujit and Sandhya, this will be the Atlas filing date. For Ajumon and Celin, this will be the date their OBA disclosures clear and their stock is actually issued (see Section 5).

**Why 4-year/1-year cliff:** Per Yokum — "this is the standard. Do not skip this because you're friends and co-founders. If any founder leaves in year one, unvested equity should not go with them. Every serious acquirer will check this and will be alarmed if it's absent."

## 4. 83(b) Election

Each Founder agrees to file a Section 83(b) election with the Internal Revenue Service **within 30 calendar days** of receiving their founder stock. This is a hard IRS deadline that cannot be extended.

The 83(b) election causes the Founder to be taxed on the value of the stock at issuance (which should be near-zero given the company's pre-formation state) rather than on the appreciation as the stock vests. Without a timely 83(b) election, the Founder will pay ordinary income tax on the value of each portion of stock as it vests over four years.

**Atlas will provide:** The 83(b) election form, instructions for filling it in, and the IRS mailing address.

**Each Founder is individually responsible for:** Mailing the 83(b) election (certified mail, return receipt requested), retaining a copy with proof of mailing, and providing a copy to the Company for the corporate records.
