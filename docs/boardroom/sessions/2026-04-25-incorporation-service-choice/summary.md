## Biggest Fight

The board agreed on Atlas but disagreed on how much mental energy this decision deserves. Yokum, Patrick, and Tonia each defended specific, non-fungible advantages (clean cap-table, pre-cleared Stripe activation, litigation bench effect). Brian and Jason argued the choice is largely industry-invisible and the founder is over-weighting Atlas-vs-Gust because it has a clear forking decision and a clear price tag — while the harder workstreams it does not address (Florida timeshare regulatory opinion, marketplace-lawyer policy review, resort-brand outreach, ARDA membership) get less attention precisely because they're harder to scope. The unanimous vote on Atlas masked this real disagreement about attention allocation.

---

## Sharpest Insight

> **Patrick McKenzie:** "Atlas is a payments-infrastructure decision wearing a corporate-formation costume." Pre-cleared Stripe live activation eliminates 2-10 days of manual underwriting friction that a brand-new timeshare-rental marketplace entity will face applying independently — that alone is worth the entire $500 fee.
