Key Tensions

1. Pre-Payment Data vs. Payment-Committed Data
Leah & Bret argue that TrustShield completion rates, search patterns, and bid behavior are valuable marketplace signals even without payments. Patrick & Simon counter that bids without payment commitment are "phantom metrics" — a traveler bidding $800 with no money on the line is expressing a preference, not demand.
2. Regulatory Probability vs. Regulatory Severity
Bret argues enforcement probability is low for a 50-user beta. Jason counters that enforcement is complaint-driven, not monitoring-driven — a single brand-protection attorney complaint triggers mandatory DBPR investigation regardless of platform size.
3. Speed vs. Clean Foundation
Casey & Leah push for maximum learning velocity — every day without users is a day the marketplace dies invisibly. Howard & Simon warn that messy early data and pre-entity operations create permanent acquisition narrative damage.
