## Biggest Fight

The real disagreement was not Florida vs. Delaware or LLC vs. C-Corp. The fight was whether the long-term VC and acquisition goal justifies optimizing the corporate structure today — even at the cost of administrative effort — or whether the founders should defer optimization until "the time is right." The board landed firmly on optimize today: the cost of starting wrong is asymmetric. A few thousand dollars and a week of attorney time today versus tens of thousands of dollars, months of friction, lost QSBS tax benefits ($9.5M+ across the founder group), and damaged first-impression signaling to acquirers and investors later.

---

## Sharpest Insight

Tonia Klausner's "adversarial readers" frame: every legal structure has multiple readers (investors, plaintiffs' lawyers, payment processors, regulators, M&A scouts, OBA monitoring systems at the co-founders' employers), and the founders' job at formation is to pick the structure that satisfies as many of those readers as possible simultaneously. The proposed structure satisfies none of them. A single Delaware C-Corp satisfies most of them.

---

## Recommended Action

Form a single Delaware C-Corporation this week with Sujit and Sandhya as the only initial stockholders (800,000 shares to founders, 200,000 reserved as option pool, 1,000,000 authorized). File 83(b) elections within 30 days — this window is absolute. Execute a four-founder agreement documenting Ajumon and Celin's promised equity contingent on documented OBA clearance from their employers — this protects their expectation interest without creating an OBA violation. Retain a Florida-licensed timeshare attorney now to commission written legal opinions on Florida Chapter 721 (timeshare resale service registration) and Chapter 560 (money transmitter licensing) before opening to real customers. Register the Delaware C-Corp as a foreign entity in Florida. Submit the Stripe Connect application under the clean C-Corp identity — do not let the proposed multi-tier structure ever touch the payments stack. Apply for ARDA membership in the next 90 days.

The cost of doing this correctly today is approximately $5,000–$10,000 in legal fees plus a few hundred dollars in state filing fees. The cost of starting with the proposed structure and unwinding it later is $50,000–$200,000 plus operational friction with payment processors, potential lost QSBS tax exclusion at exit, and damaged first impressions with industry acquirers.

---

## Dissent Worth Heeding

Brian Lower's original instinct — that Florida Chapter 721 timeshare regulatory exposure is more important than the corporate structure debate — must not get lost in the structural recommendation. The cleanest Delaware C-Corp in the world does not save RAV from operating an unregistered timeshare resale service in Florida. Get the legal opinion in writing, on letterhead, from a Florida-licensed timeshare attorney, before processing the first transaction. Brian's caution is the conscience of this debate even though his vote ultimately moved.
